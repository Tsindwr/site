## Invention

Empir Draconi was a hub of ingenuity, blending arcane mastery with practical engineering. The empire pioneered advancements in levitation technology, adaptive metallurgy, and elemental energy manipulation. Many of these innovations revolved around the needs of their floating cities, war efforts, and trade networks.

---

## Resources

### Distribution

The empire’s economy depended on a structured resource distribution network. Floating cities relied on docking settlements and ground-based provinces for raw materials, while centralized bureaucracies managed supply chains with magical oversight.

### Imports

Despite its self-sufficiency, Empir Draconi imported specialized resources, including:

- **Rare minerals** from deep-earth mines and neighboring nations.
- **Exotic alchemical reagents** traded from distant lands.
- **Luxury textiles** woven from foreign arcane silks.

### Income

The empire's wealth flowed from multiple avenues, including:

- **Taxation** of settlements and guilds.
- **Arcane trade** in spellwork, artifacts, and energy refinement.
- **Military conquest and subjugation**, seizing assets from lesser nations.
- **Guild monopolies** over essential crafts, enforced through imperial decree.

---

## Key Industries

> [!abstract]- **Aethercrafting**
> The refinement and shaping of aetherstone and other levitation materials, crucial for maintaining the floating cities.

> [!abstract]- **Elemental Energy Harnessing**
> Extraction and containment of raw elemental forces for power sources and weaponry.

> [!abstract]- **Artificing and Enchantment**
> The empire's skilled artificers infused weapons, armor, and tools with arcane properties, leading to a booming enchanted goods trade.

> [!abstract]- **Draconic Alchemy**
> Harnessing dragon blood, scales, and essence for medicinal, magical, and military applications.

## Professions

### Niche Professions

> [!abstract]- **Fabric Engineers**
> Experts who studied and manipulated [[The Arras]] to stabilize floating cities and arcane structures.

> [!abstract]- **Skybound Merchants**
> Traders specializing in high-altitude commerce, piloting wind-skiffs and aerial caravans between cities.

> [!abstract]- **Rune-Scribes**
> Scholars and artisans who engraved arcane runes into tools, weapons, and architecture to enhance their properties.

> [!abstract]- **Tidecallers**
> Mages who regulated water flow to prevent oceanic disasters before the empire’s fall.

### Companies

Several powerful trade syndicates and noble-backed enterprises controlled different sectors of the economy:

- **The Gilded Wyrm Consortium** – A merchant guild overseeing the trade of enchanted goods and arcane relics.
- **Draconis Aetherworks** – The empire’s leading manufacturer of aetherstone-based technologies.
- **The Obsidian Exchange** – A financial institution managing imperial wealth and high-value transactions.
- **The Talon Forges** – A state-backed weapons manufacturer specializing in enchanted arms for the military.
- **The Azure Loom** – A luxury textile company producing enchanted fabrics for nobility and spellcasters alike.
