## Trade Partners

- **The Aetherbound Consortium** – A powerful mercantile network that specialized in the trade of arcane materials, rare metals, and enchanted textiles. Their airships frequently docked at Empir Draconi’s floating cities, exchanging resources for exclusive access to the empire’s aetherstone reserves.
- **The Myrmidic Holds** – A collection of fortified enclaves along the empire’s eastern frontier, known for their exceptional craftsmanship in weapons and armor. Empir Draconi relied on their metallurgy and, in return, provided protective enchantments and air transport.
- **The Pearl Cities of the Azure Coast** – A federation of seafaring city-states that engaged in the trade of exotic spices, aquatic alchemy components, and luxury goods. The empire exchanged enchanted constructs and navigational artifacts for their maritime expertise.
- **The Obsidian League** – A shadowy yet influential trade network that dealt in rare artifacts, alchemical reagents, and contraband. Though officially denounced by the empire, many within its nobility relied on their smuggling operations to circumvent trade restrictions.

## Allies

- **The Skyward Compact** – A coalition of floating city-states that aligned with Empir Draconi in mutual defense, technological exchange, and cultural collaboration. Their bond ensured shared advancements in aerial navigation and levitation magic.
- **The Covenant of Dawn** – A religious order devoted to celestial phenomena, whose healers and diviners worked closely with the empire’s magisters. Their alliance bolstered the empire’s spiritual legitimacy and access to divine magic.
- **The Argent Dominion** – A knightly order dedicated to preserving ancient relics and protecting arcane knowledge. Their warriors often served as elite guards in Empir Draconi’s highest courts and arcane academies.
- **The Ember Pact** – A diplomatic agreement between fire-wielding elemental clans and the empire, ensuring cooperation in energy production, warfare, and trade of rare volcanic minerals.

### Drakropoles Sorori

#### **1. Vorthessar & Vorthasdral**

- **Vorthessar** (Floating) – Known as the “Crown of the Magisters,” this city was home to the most prestigious arcane academies and research sanctums. It housed the Magisterium’s grand council chambers, where laws were debated and the empire’s magical advancements were overseen.
- **Vordral** (Docking) – A fortified settlement built into the mountain base, Vordral served as the gateway to Vorthessar, hosting alchemical markets, smithies specializing in arcane-infused weaponry, and diplomatic lodges for emissaries.

#### **2. Xiranthessar & Xirandral**

- **Xiranthessar** (Floating) – A stronghold of the empire’s military elite, Xiranthessar functioned as a mobile command center, allowing rapid deployment of imperial forces. Its sky-docks were lined with drake-rider garrisons and warforged constructs, ready for battle at a moment’s notice.
- **Xirandral** (Docking) – A rugged outpost carved into the cliffs below, Xirandral was a recruitment hub, prison complex, and proving ground for warriors. Its infamous gladiatorial pits tested potential conscripts in brutal combat trials.

#### **3. Zevorithessar & Zevoridral**

- **Zevorithessar** (Floating) – The heart of commerce and trade, Zevorithessar’s shimmering marketplaces were famed across the empire, dealing in rare goods, magical artifacts, and luxury wares. Merchant princes wielded immense influence here, competing for imperial favor.
- **Zevoridral** (Docking) – A heavily defended checkpoint ensuring only authorized traders could access Zevorithessar. It was also a haven for spies and clandestine dealings, as ambitious merchants often sought to undercut rivals through underhanded means.

#### **4. Aerynthessar & Aeryndral**

- **Aerynthessar** (Floating) – Revered as the “City of Saints,” Aerynthessar was the spiritual center of Empir Draconi, where high priests communed with celestial forces and the doctrine of the empire’s divine mandate was solidified.
- **Aeryndral** (Docking) – A sprawling sanctuary and pilgrimage site, Aeryndral housed shrines, temples, and healing wards where both the faithful and the desperate sought solace or divine intervention.

#### **5. Dravethessar & Dravedral**

- **Dravethessar** (Floating) – An industrial and technological marvel, Dravethessar was home to the empire’s most advanced forges and artificer workshops. The city pioneered soulforging techniques and produced enchanted armaments of unparalleled craftsmanship.
- **Dravedral** (Docking) – A mining and labor settlement that supplied raw materials to Dravethessar’s forges. Populated largely by skilled laborers and bound artisans, Dravedral was the backbone of the empire’s weapon production and infrastructure.

## Enemies

- **The Titansworn Clans** – The remnants of the giants who waged the Titanfall Conflict, seeking vengeance against the draconic rulers for their ancient war crimes. They frequently raided imperial territories and disrupted trade routes.
- **The Gloamborn Rebellion** – A secretive insurgency that opposed the empire’s expansionist policies and arcane exploitation. Their agents worked from the shadows, sabotaging imperial projects and inciting uprisings among the oppressed.
- **The House of Ash** – A rogue faction of exiled Draconi nobles and arcane dissidents who sought to reclaim lost power through subterfuge and assassination. They maintained hidden enclaves in distant lands, plotting their return.
- **The Umbral Dominion** – A rival empire that vied for control over arcane ley lines and forbidden knowledge. Their necromantic overlords viewed Empir Draconi as both a threat and a prize to be conquered.

---

## Neighbours

- **The Verdant Principalities** – A loose confederation of forested kingdoms to the west, known for their deep druidic traditions and reluctance to engage with Empir Draconi’s arcane pursuits. Trade existed, but tensions over deforestation and resource exploitation remained high.
- **The Sunken Halls of Nythra** – An ancient subterranean civilization dwelling within the Kagecombs, whose relationship with the empire was one of cautious diplomacy. Their expertise in abyssal magic and underground navigation made them valuable allies and dangerous foes.
- **The Stormforged Bastions** – Mountain strongholds inhabited by tempest-wielding warrior clans, some of whom served as mercenaries while others actively resisted imperial expansion.
- **The Whispering Sands Confederacy** – A desert-dwelling network of nomadic tribes that controlled key trade routes across the former seabed. Their neutrality made them indispensable as guides, but their loyalty was never guaranteed.
